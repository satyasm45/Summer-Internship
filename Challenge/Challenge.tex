\section{Challenge Question 1}
Show that the matrix $(t\vec{I}-\vec{n}\vec{n}^T)$ in the given document is a rank 1 matrix for a parabola where t=$\norm{\vec{n}}^2$
.
%
\section{Explanation}
Given :
\begin{align}
    \vec{V} = (t\vec{I}-\vec{n}\vec{n}^T) 
\end{align}
where t=$\norm{\vec{n}}^2$.
Now $\vec{V}$ is a $2\times2$ matrix.Using Rank-Nullity theorem:
Rank($\vec{V}$)+Nullity($\vec{V}$)=2
\begin{align}
  \vec{V}\neq\vec{0}\label{eq:2}\\
  \mydet{\vec{V}}=0\label{eq:3}
\end{align}
\eqref{eq:2} indicates Rank($\vec{V}$)$\neq0$ and \eqref{eq:3} indicates Nullity($\vec{V}$)$\neq0$ .So, Rank($\vec{V}$)=Nullity($\vec{V}$)=1 for parabola.For other conics:
\begin{align}
 t=\frac{\norm{\vec{n}}^2}{e^2}\\
 \mydet{\vec{V}}\neq0\label{eq:5}
\end{align}
\eqref{eq:5} indicates Nullity($\vec{V}$)=0 as $\vec{V}$ is invertible.So,for conics other than parabola Rank($\vec{V}$)=2.
